About

I recently graduated from City, University of London with a Class I in BSc Psychology. While I was completing my university degree, I started dabbling with HTML and CSS when I could find the time - unfortunately, as I was working 25-60% while studying full-time, I couldn't dedicate as much time to it as I would've liked, but I completed online tutorials through Codecademy and codebar and attended workshops when I could.

It wasn't until the beginning of this autumn, after I'd finished my degree and my current job was coming to an end, that I realised that I now had the chance to seriously pursue a career as a web developer. So, during the past two months I have brushed up on my HTML and CSS skills, and during the past month I have given myself a crash course in JavaScript and worked on meeting the prerequisites for the Founders and Coders course in London.

I love programming and the fact that it gives me the ability to create things, and be creative and use logic to solve challenging problems — not to mention that there’s always more to learn! It’s something that I get a lot of energy from, and it would be amazing to have it as a career!

My experiences of the community here in London and the one surrounding Founders and Coders has been great, it’s such an encouraging and helpful community, and it’s just one more thing that makes me excited about making a move into this industry. I have had so much use of workshops and networking events organised by organisations within the community (see the Community section to find out more about these organisations) and I would love to become more involved with organisations like these once I have aquired the necessary programming skills.

Community

There are a lot of great organisations in London who help people who want to learn how to code. Some of these organisations are set up specifically for underrepresented groups in the tech community, whereas others are for everyone.

I highly recommend getting ivolved with them either as a mentor or a mentee!

codebar London

codebar is a non-profit initiative that facilitates the growth of a diverse tech community by running regular programming workshops.

Their goal is to enable underrepresented groups to learn programming in a safe and collaborative environment and expand their career opportunities. To achieve this, they run free weekly workshops and regular events for identify as women or LGBTQ, and people from underrepresented minority groups.

You can find out more about them and sign up their workshops on their website.

Node Girls London

Node Girls runs free bootcamps in JavaScript and Node.js for women, non-binary and trans folk. They were inspired by Rails Girls and Django Girls to create something awesome for the JavaScript community, and on top of the London chapter, there are also chapters running around the world.

You can find out more about them and sign up their workshops on their website.

Founders & Coders

Founders & Coders is a UK-based nonprofit organization that runs a tuition-free, peer-led coding academy in London. They give back to the community by providing technical expertise and training to nonprofits, local businesses, and early-stage startups. As a part of this, they host weekly meet-ups where anyone can come and work on their projects with other coders.

You can read out more about them on their website and find out more about their meet-ups on their Meetup page.

Code First: Girls

Code First: Girls works with companies and with women to help increase the number of women in tech.

They believe that tech shouldn't just be a boys club, and they tackle the issue by supporting young and working age women to develop further personal and professional skills, and connect women to a community of other talented and like minded women and companies who can support and accompany them through their professional development.

You can find out more about them on their website.
